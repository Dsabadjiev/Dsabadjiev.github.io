Community Service

Pi Day Math Tournament

The annual AMSA-MAMS Pi Day Math Tournament (PDMT) is an all-day math competition for middle school students around Pi Day and organized by high school students from Mass Academy and AMSA. This was the third year that the competition ran, and I wrote problems for the competition both this year and last year. Working with my peers to write problems for middle schoolers was surprisingly fun, and it was also different to be on the problem-writing end, as I am usually the one doing the problems. This year I also took a more active roll and was head proctor, which meant that I directed students participating in the competition. PDMT was an overall great experience, and it was really fun to work with kids and help with the event.

Extending Water Root

Various people expressed interest in my group’s STEM II project, so we are building more devices so that more people can use our device. Though the process of making more mechanical devices is straightforward, integrating the devices with separate applications will require additional work. Currently, the app is directly connected to a firebase database which connects to an Arduino, but for another device we would probably have to make another app and link it with a new database so that the apps control different devices. We could also extend the project to find a more elegant solution to multiple devices or expand the functionality.

Worcester Tree Initiative

This summer I also plan to volunteer at the Worcester Tree Initiative. The Worcester Tree Initiative is an organization to promote planting and caring for trees in Worcester. It began with replanting the trees lost in Worcester to the Asian Longhorned Beetle, but grew into an organization to make Worcester’s forests better than before and provide the natural benefits of a forest in the city. I am planning on helping with weeding and mulching this summer.
