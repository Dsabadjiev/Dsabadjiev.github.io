STEM 1

For STEM I, everyone has an independent research project, and my project was on integrating sonification into education, specifically into a lesson on joint and combined variation. Sonification is the process of translating data into sound to generate “sound graphs” or audio feedback. My hope was that these sound graphs would help students get a feel for joint and combined variation the way visual graphs can help students understand direct and inverse variation and would be more effective than visual graphs because joint and combined variation handles multivariate data. For my testing, I had a pair of google forms which each had a couple surveys, tests, and a video lesson on joint and combined variation. Click here to see the form with the lesson with sonification. I tested 20 juniors from Mass Academy and 18 seventh graders from AMSA. Though the pretest and posttest scores for AMSA students suggested that sonification may lead to better improvement, I did not have enough data to show a significant link. In mid-December, we had a practice science fair where everyone presented their research up to that point, and in mid-February we had a local science fair where we presented our projects to a panel of judges and to the public.
