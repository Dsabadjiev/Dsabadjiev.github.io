STEM 2

For STEM II, everyone has a group project designing an assistive device to help a client with a disability. I worked in a group of three to make the process of watering a kitchen house plant accessible by designing a garden maintenance system that would promote gardening independence for people with limited mobility. My group combined our apps for good project and our STEM II project, so we integrated software with hardware. The hardware consisted of the mechanical device which delivers water to a plant via a showerhead and the physical electronics to power and control the device, while the software consisted of an Android application and Arduino code to control the electronics. I worked mainly on the electronics and processing data with Firebase, a cloud database service. A picture of the device is shown to the right and a pdf of the poster is shown below.

The electronics consisted of a wall adapter that provided 12 V DC to power a circuit which then powered a NodeMCU board. The board was in turn connected to a pump and a moisture sensor. The board accessed Firebase and powered the pump and reported the moisture when commanded by certain Firebase variables. A diagram of the electronics is shown to the left. The mechanical portion consisted of a Lexan plate attached to a stand with a showerhead. The showerhead was designed to distribute water throughout the plant.
